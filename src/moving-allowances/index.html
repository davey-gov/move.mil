--- layout: page title: Moving Allowances (Entitlements) Overview ---

Moving allowances, also known as entitlements, with the U.S. Military are largely determined based on the service member’s or civilian’s rank and dependency status. Additionally, special circumstances like a OCONUS move (moving overseas), retirement move, or separation move may have extra entitlements and/or limitations than the standard PCS (Permanent Change of Station) move within the continental United States. The Joint Travel Regulation (JTR) is the authority of entitlements for all service members, although each branch of the services may also have supplemental regulations to the JTR.

As questions arise, your Transportation Office (TO) can provide further information and generally is the best place to get help related to your move.

Military Terms Explained

CONUS (Within the Contintenal United States)

CONUS are moves that start and end within the 48 contiguous states of the US. Most moves in the US Military are CONUS moves.

OCONUS (Outside of the Continental United States)

Moves to or from Alaska and Hawaii along with international stations are considered OCONUS moves. These moves are more complicated as generally each location will have additional limitations on what you are allowed to transport.
